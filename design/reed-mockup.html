§1.4

Minimum pairwise OKLab ΔE×100, Viénot 1999. The isoluminant grid a critic called the best idea in the corpus collapses to ΔE 1.06 under deuteranopia — the same tile twice. Staggering lightness instead is the CVD fix and the weave in one decision.

What you're looking at

§3.5

Tap a tile. It lights and stays lit until speech ends. Press feedback and the speaking indicator are one signal. Tap the lit tile to stop — there is no STOP button.

§5

A STOP control was specified, sourced to an emergency-machinery standard, and deleted. It cancels TTS — that's a cancel. Coding it as a red emergency bar designs for a bystander's fear of the user.

§6.1

Switch to “show”, then tap a tile. Each line is scaled independently to touch both margins — the most beautiful setting and the most legible one are the same computation. Tap anywhere to exit.

§1.3

Cycle the theme (top-left): paper → ink → high contrast. Three positions, four palettes — a shutdown user needs one tap to produce one predictable next state.

§3.7

One slot is empty: ground, nothing. Not a hole and not broken — a socket with nothing installed.

§3.8

Tab through the grid. The ring sits in the gutter, never on the fill, and traversal follows priority, not layout — “I can’t talk” is first, though it sits in the lower-centre arc.
