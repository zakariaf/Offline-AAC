Correct.

WHAT FAILS (and it is the part the design decision rests on):

1. "Pullin's axis is discretion vs. DISCUSSION" — FABRICATED. No such axis exists. The book is built from seven explicitly named "X meets Y" tension chapters: Fashion meets discretion; Exploring meets solving; Simple meets universal; Identity meets ability; Provocative meets sensitive; Feeling meets testing; Expression meets information. The word "discussion" is not the pole of anything. Pullin's opposing term to discretion is FASHION. The book poses the question "if discretion were to be challenged as a priority, what would take its place?" and answers it with fashion/design culture — not with conversation-provocation. Nor does "design for discussion" appear in Pullin's later Dundee work (Studio Ordinary, Hands of X, "17 ways to say yes," Imagining Technologies for Disability Futures). Someone invented a memorable alliteration and attributed it to him.

2. "The lesson is CONFIDENT VISIBILITY, not concealment" — INVERTS PULLIN. This is the single most damaging error, because Pullin explicitly forecloses the binary. Direct quote, Design Meets Disability p. 48: "Fashion can be understated, and discretion does not require invisibility." Pullin is not anti-discretion. He is against discretion being the automatic and only priority. He attacks the CONFLATION of discretion with invisibility — the claim reintroduces exactly that conflation, just with the sign flipped.

3. "Carry the app's whole aesthetic argument at full volume" — CONTRADICTED BY PULLIN'S OWN IDEAL. His stated design target (quoted in a peer-reviewed source as Pullin 2018, p. 47) is a "super normal" hearing aid: "beautifully resolved in form, materials and details — to the degree that eyewear is — yet unmistakably, unashamedly and unremarkably a hearing aid." Note UNREMARKABLY. Pullin's eyewear lesson is that glasses won by becoming ORDINARY and well-made, not by shouting. "Full volume" is the maximalist reading his "understated" and "unremarkably" language specifically rules out.

4. Source rot: the claimed MIT Press URL (mitpress.mit.edu/books/design-meets-disability) is dead — 403. Canonical is https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262516747/design-meets-disability/. The claimed Core77 URL is missing its trailing "-15597" slug.

THE IRONY: the claim's own rhetorical punchline is "Most people cite Pullin and then build the thing he argues against." The claim does precisely this. It cites Pullin and derives "full volume aesthetic argument" from an author who wrote that discretion does not require invisibility and whose ideal object is "unremarkable."

DESIGN IMPACT: Show Mode as a deliberate design surface is a defensible idea and Pullin supports taking it seriously rather than treating it as a utility readout. But Pullin cannot be used as authority for maximalism on that screen, and "discretion vs. discussion" must not appear in the corpus as his framing. If the corpus wants a discussion/conversation-provocation axis, that is a different literature (critical/discursive design) and needs its own citation — attributing it to Pullin is a fabrication that will not survive review.
