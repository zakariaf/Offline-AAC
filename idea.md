Dignified offline AAC for adults & teens with situational speech loss


Who it's for: Autistic adults and teens who go non-speaking during shutdowns/meltdowns/sensory overload, plus people with selective mutism, aphasia, or post-seizure speech loss. They gather in r/autism, r/AutisticAdults, r/selectivemutism, and AAC communities.
The problem: Every mainstream AAC app is built for young children — cartoon avatars, "parental" account gates, kiddie vocabulary — which is infantilizing for the adults who need it, so they abandon it. Premium options (Proloquo2Go/TouchChat/LAMP) run ~$299 and iOS-only.
Why offline is essential: It's a disability accommodation, not a networked service. The user must be able to "speak" instantly — in a shop, an ER, a car with no signal, mid-shutdown — with zero login, zero loading, and full privacy. Any dependency on connectivity is a failure at the exact moment of need.
Why they're underserved: The gap is audience and dignity, not technology. No one has built the adult-first, tasteful, offline version; existing apps assume the user is an 8-year-old.
The core job: Tap a phrase/symbol tile (or type) and the phone speaks it aloud, instantly, offline, with an adult-appropriate design and no account.
What the MVP does: A grid of large, customizable phrase tiles + a "type to speak" box + on-device text-to-speech. Editable categories. No sign-up. Nothing leaves the device. Dark, calm, adult visual design.
Stays simple?: Yes — one screen, one job. Real risks: TTS must sound acceptable (OS voices are fine); design must feel adult without being cold; must be usable one-handed by someone in distress.

Confidence: High. Verified pain across independent sources, a specific reachable community, one genuinely focused job, and a gap generalists keep missing because they default to kid-AAC.
