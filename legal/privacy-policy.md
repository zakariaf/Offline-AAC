# Reed — Privacy Policy

*Effective 16 July 2026. Published by Zakaria Fatahi.*

Reed is a text-to-speech board for part-time AAC. This policy explains what Reed does and does not do with your information. It is written to be checked, not only read. The current hosted version lives at <https://reed.applander.io/privacy>.

## Reed collects no personal data

Reed has no accounts and no sign-up. It has no analytics, no advertising, no crash-reporting service, and no third-party SDKs that report on you. The phrases you write and the settings you choose are stored on your device, in Reed's own storage. Reed does not send them to a server, because Reed has no server.

## How speech works

Reed has no network code and no network permission. Speech is synthesized by your device's own system text-to-speech engine, and Reed only selects voices that declare they need no network. That engine is a separate app, provided by your device or its manufacturer, running under its own identity and its own permissions. When you tap a tile or type a sentence, Reed hands that text to the system engine so it can be spoken aloud. Reed cannot inspect or control what that engine does, which is why Reed filters for voices that report they work without a network rather than claiming to verify it.

## No network permission on Android

On Android, Reed ships without the INTERNET permission. This is enforced by the operating system, and it is listed on the Google Play page before you install. You can confirm Reed's central privacy claim without installing Reed and without trusting this document. A store privacy label is declared by the developer; the permission list is a fact the platform holds.

iOS offers no equivalent, platform-verified assurance that an app cannot reach the network. On iPhone and iPad, this claim rests on Reed having no network code, not on an operating-system promise.

## Your board and backups

On Android, Reed turns automatic cloud backup off, so your board is not copied to Google Drive. Moving your board to a new phone is done through the export in Reed's settings — a step you start yourself.

On iOS, whether on-device files are included in an iCloud backup is decided by the operating system. Apple describes an app's request to exclude a file as guidance only, not a promise. Reed asks the system to keep your board out of iCloud backup, but iOS does not promise it. If you would rather your board not be backed up at all, you can turn off iCloud backup for Reed in your iPhone settings. iCloud backups are held by Apple under Apple's privacy policy; Reed has no access to them.

## Your board stays yours

Because Reed has no account and no server, there is no entitlement to expire and nothing to shut down. Reed keeps working if its developer stops maintaining it. To remove what Reed has stored, delete tiles in the app or uninstall Reed; uninstalling removes Reed's on-device data from that device.

## Children's privacy

Reed is a general-audience tool intended for adults and is not directed at children. Reed collects no personal information from anyone, including children.
